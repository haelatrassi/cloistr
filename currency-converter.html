How currency conversion works

  • Exchange rates change throughout the day based on global markets.
  • The rates shown here come from the European Central Bank and update once per day.
  • For actual transactions, banks and money transfer services add a margin to the official rate.

Why exchange rates differ between providers

The "mid-market rate" you see in our tool is the average between buy and sell prices on the wholesale currency market. Banks and currency exchange services add a markup (typically 2-5%) on top, plus fees. Services like Wise and Revolut tend to charge less, while traditional banks and airport exchanges charge more.

FAQ

Are these rates live?

Rates come from the European Central Bank and update once per day. They are the mid-market rates used in global currency trading.

Why is the bank rate different?

Banks and exchange services add a markup of 2-5% plus fees to the mid-market rate. That is how they make money on currency conversion.
